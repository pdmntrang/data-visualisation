SUICIDE RATE 2009 - 2019

Death by suicide is a conscious topic that causes extreme pain to people around the world every year. The World Health Organisation provided this data to prevent and control the suicide rate for informational and research purposes.

Global distribution of suicide

In 2019, the global suicide rate was 9 per 100,000 population.

In most countries, the suicide rate decreased over time. China suicide rate dropped by 3.42 per 100,000 population over the past ten years. However, Australia and the United States suicide rate increased over time by 1.24 and 3.09 per 100,000 population, respectively.

Suicide rate in different region

How has the suicide rate changed over the past ten years?

From 2009 to 2019, Africa has the highest average suicide rate, with Lesotho has the highest world suicide rate per 100,000 population.

Overall, countries with lower GDP per capita have higher suicide rates than countries with high incomes.

In 2009, the average suicide rate in South-East Asia was higher than Americas, but in 2019, the rate in South-East Asia dropped below the Americas.

Sri Lanka was in the top 30 countries with the highest suicide rate in 2009, but the rate decreased by 9.83 per 100,000 population after ten years.
